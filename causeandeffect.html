CAUSES AND EFFECTS
THE HUNGER CYCLE
Hunger exists in a cycle called the hunger cycle. The hunger cycle is a cycle of poverty that many people are currently trapped in. Malnourished mothers give birth to undernourished babies. These babies grow up with not enough food which could lead to physical stunting and a slower mental development. If these children can afford to go to school, chronic health issues prevent them from doing well and gaining a proper education. The lack of education as a child prevents adults from finding a sustainable job, becoming trapped in poverty. If the adult starts a family, they will not have enough money to provide for it, causing the cycle to repeat.
CAUSES
POVERTY
Over 1/3 of the world's population live in poverty, everyday these people have to spend 60-80% of their income on food. Often due to financial difficulties, these people have to skip many meals.
NATURAL DISASTER
Half of the world's population grow their own food. Natural disasters cut off food chains, resulting in a lack of food. Events such as droughts can cause a decline in food security.
CLIMATE CHANGE
Climate change causes weather shocks that can cut off food chains.
CONFLICT
The prevelance of hunger in lower-middle income regions is 4% higher than the prvealence in other lower-middle income regions. 489 million of the 815 million people suffering from hunger are living through a conflict. Conflict can affect food and agricultural production.
POLITICAL INSTABILITY
Political instability can cause decrease of the economy, which causes decrease in the value of currency. This leads to an increase in poverty.
FOOD & AGRICULTURAL VALUE
The effectiveness of food production varies based on the region. Areas with less effective production have less food available for the community.
EFFECTS
UNDERNUTRITION
Undernutrition and fetal growth restrictions cause 45% of all child deaths annually, or 3.1 million deaths. Undernutrition can include both wasting and stunting. Wasting is defined as having a low weight for the individual's height, 50.5 milion children under 5 are currently wasted. 2/3 of children wasted are living in Asia, and 1/4 in Africa. Stunting is defined as having a height below average for the individual's age. In 2017, 150 million children were estimated to be stunted, however the prevalence of stunting has decreased by 11% from 2007-2017.
